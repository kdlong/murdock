The Course:
Astr 1010 is an introductory astronomy course, emphasizing the way in which the scientific method is applied to the study of the universe. It is a non--technical course, entirely suitable for general education science requirements or any student interested in the subject matter; the mathematical level will be kept as low as possible in showing how scientists have developed their ideas about the universe.
The purpose of the course is to give an appreciation for astronomy, scientific method and the quantitative nature of science, and to give an understanding of the physical universe.

Text
Michael Seeds, Foundations of Astronomy, 7th ed.

Prerequisites:
Rudimentary high school algebra and geometry. (No logarithms or trigonometry should be necessary.) Previous introduction to the natural sciences will be helpful.

Other requirements:
Students will be required to use a scientific calculator to study the course material, and for the exams. By ``scientific calculator'' is meant one which can work with scientific notation and has ``power'' and 10x functions. Generally, a calculator which has the keys sin, cos and tan is satisfactory (although we will not use these functions). It need not cost more than $12 and can be purchased in stores where I buy my wardrobe, i.e. those whose names end in the syllable ``Mart'', or in them big office supply stores.

Workload
Two midterm tests and a final exam. Reports on laboratory exercises. Several problem sets will be assigned to be handed in, but others will be only suggested with answers given later in class. We will not have a laboratory exercises every week, but they are a required portion of the course for which you have registered at the given time slot. A ``make-up'' for a missed lab will only be considered for important, last-minute emergencies.

Grading
Grades will be based on the exams, with a 25%--25%--30% weight for them (respectively) and 25% on lab reports and 10% on required problem sets. Final grades will be based on the best ``curve'' I feel is justified, but I will guarantee at least the following grade scale for the total possible points:

F: 0-30 D: 31-50 C: 51-65 B: 66-85 A: 86-100

Attendance is not required, but is strongly suggested since the exams will cover the material which I discuss in class, and in places this does not follow the order of the textbook. Students are responsible for knowing about all announcements made in class.

Topics
Topics for 1010 will include: The appearance and "motions" of the sky; the history of astronomy; gravity and planetary motion; what we know about the individual planets; other members of the solar system (excluding the Sum).
